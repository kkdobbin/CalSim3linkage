**Santa Monica-City, Water Division (CA1910146)**  
**CCR report year: 2023**  

The Santa Monica-City Water Division relies on two primary sources of water: local groundwater and imported surface water.

**Source 1: Local Groundwater**  
**Source Type**: Groundwater  
**Where It Comes From**: Extracted from local groundwater wells within the City and in West Los Angeles, specifically from three sub-basins within the Santa Monica Basin (Charnock, Olympic, and Arcadia Sub-basins).  
**Fraction of Total Annual Water Supply**: 75%  
**Notes**: The Charnock Sub-basin has been a local supply since 1924. The water is treated at the Arcadia Water Treatment Plant, which includes processes such as iron/manganese removal, filtration, reverse osmosis softening, aeration, disinfection, and fluoridation. The Charnock Well Field Restoration Project is in place to treat contaminants like MTBE and TBA.

**Source 2: Imported Surface Water**  
**Source Type**: Surface Water  
**Where It Comes From**: Purchased from the Metropolitan Water District (MWD), which imports water from two sources: the Colorado River (delivered from Lake Havasu through a 242-mile-long aqueduct) and the State Water Project (delivered through the 441-mile-long California Aqueduct originating in the Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta).  
**Fraction of Total Annual Water Supply**: 25%  
**Notes**: The imported water is treated at MWD's treatment plants (Weymouth and Jensen Plants) before being blended with locally produced water and distributed to residents. 

This summary highlights the reliance on local groundwater for the majority of the water supply, with a significant portion also coming from imported sources to ensure a sustainable and safe drinking water supply for the community.
